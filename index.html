Dazzel is a 3D social platform built for people who want more than scrolling feeds and endless notifications.


It’s a place to actually hang out online—stream live, share your creativity, and connect with others in a way that feels real.
Whether you’re jamming to music with friends, watching videos together, or hosting virtual concerts and meetups, everything happens in an immersive 3D space that runs right in your browser with zero extra hardware.
Group video calls in Dazzel aren’t just boxes on a screen—they happen inside a virtual environment where you can move around, chat, and interact naturally.
Starting a call here is faster and easier than launching Google Meet or Zoom.

Use cases go beyond casual hangouts.
Creators can livestream their art, DJs can throw virtual parties, and gamers can organize meetups without leaving the browser.
Remote teams get a 3D space for meetings and brainstorming sessions that actually feels collaborative instead of just another conference call.
Musicians can host virtual jam sessions, and event organizers can build digital venues for concerts or panels, open to anyone around the world.

Dazzel isn’t just chasing the Metaverse buzzword—we built it before that hype wave and focused on what actually works for users.
We want to bring back the excitement the early internet had, where discovery, creativity, and real connections mattered.
Dazzel is about creating digital worlds where people want to spend time, meet others, and share experiences that don’t feel fake or forced.

No clunky VR gear, no installs, just a quick link and you’re inside.
